## Chicken Breast
- **Macros**: 300 Cal, 56gP per Breast
- **Notes**: I get these in bulk from Costco

## Ground Beef
- **Macros**: 726 Cal, 90.8gP per 1lbs
- **Notes**: Ground beef with a fat content of <= 9%. I can find this often at Sunterra, but in bulk I prefer to use Extra Lean Ground Bison, which has similar macros

## Extra Lean Ground Bison
- **Macros**: 817 Cal, 90.8gP per 1lbs
- **Notes**: These are sold in 3 individual 1lbs packs at Costco. With only 10% fat, the macros are close enough to extra lean ground beef that I substitute it for this most of the time.

## Rice
- **Macros**: 150 Cal, 3gP per 45g
- **Notes**: I use short-grain sushi rice for most Japanese recipes, and use long-grain, basmati rice for everything else. The macros are the same

## Red Lentil Pasta
- **Macros**: 290 Cal, 22gP per 85g dry
- **Notes**: Specifically the felicia brand from Costco

## Protein Pasta
- **Macros**: 290 Cal, 20gP per 85g dry
- **Notes**: Chickpea or Red Lentil pasta from anywhere other than the felicia brand. This will generally be more expensive with slightly less protein

## Pasta
- **Macros**: 300 Cal, 12gP per 85g dry
- **Notes**: Shape does not affect macros

## Top Sirloin Steak
- **Macros**: 450 Cal, 78gP per lbs, uncooked
- **Notes**: I buy these from Costco in bulk when I need to dice them

## Thinly Sliced Strip Loin
- **Macros**: 1600 Cal, 250gP per 2.5lbs
- **Notes**: Costco will sell these but only sometimes. I'm not sure how i got the macros for this so it might be inaccurate

## Eggs
- **Macros**: 40 Cal, 7gP per egg
- **Notes**: it's an egg.

## Cheese
- **Macros**: 120 Cal, 7gP per 30g
- **Notes**: All cheese has the same macros, except for Parmesan which is weirdly high in protein

## Parmesan Cheese
- **Macros**: 130 Cal, 12gP per 30g
- **Notes**: You can substitute Grana Padano and Parmeggiano Reggiano interchangeably. I don't know the difference

## Japanese Curry
- **Macros**: 540 Cal, 6gP per 1/2 of the entire box (4 roux cubes)
- **Notes**: All of the premade rouxs have identical macros but I go with Golden or Royal Curry from T&T. Glico is widely available but I find it to be a little paste-y
